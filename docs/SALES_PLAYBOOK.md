# Sales Playbook

## Sales Objective

Get the first 5 paying restaurant customers as fast as possible by selling a margin audit and then converting the audit into recurring software usage.

This is not a volume funnel at the start. It is founder-led sales with brutal qualification.

## Ideal Early Customer

- owner-operated restaurant
- 10-100 seats
- menu is stable enough to analyze
- has at least rough recipe and price data
- feels margin pressure
- can make buying decisions directly

Avoid early:

- chains with complex approval
- restaurants with no usable recipe data at all
- businesses asking for full POS or inventory replacement

## Core Pitch

English:

"You already have sales data. You probably also have recipes somewhere. What you do not have is a clear answer to which dishes are making you money and which are quietly killing margin. We show you exactly where the leaks are and what action to take next."

Estonian cold opener:

"Kas sul on 10 min, et naitan kus sa menus raha kaotad?"

## First Sales Script

1. Open with margin loss, not software.
2. Ask how they currently price dishes and track food cost.
3. Find whether decisions are still driven by gut feel or spreadsheets.
4. Offer a free or low-cost mini audit using 2-3 real dishes.
5. Show one uncomfortable truth fast.
6. Convert the shock into a paid next step.

## Qualification Questions

- How do you currently know which dishes are most profitable?
- When ingredient prices change, how fast do you see the effect on menu margin?
- Do you have written recipes with quantities for your best-selling dishes?
- Who decides price changes?
- How often do you review menu profitability now?
- What happens if one top-selling dish loses margin for a month?
- Would you pay to see a clear weekly action list instead of raw reports?

## Audit Offer

Offer:

- free mini audit for 2-3 dishes
- or paid deeper setup audit if interest is real

Mini audit deliverable:

- dish cost estimate
- margin estimate
- one action recommendation
- estimated monthly impact

Goal of audit:

- prove the pain
- prove the product logic
- test willingness to act

## Demo Flow

1. Input 2-3 real dishes
2. Show true ingredient cost and dish margin
3. Highlight one high-sales low-margin item
4. Simulate a price change or recipe tweak
5. Show monthly profit delta
6. Ask for buying signal

Close question:

"Kui see tootaks kogu menuu peal, kas maksaksid 49 eurot kuus?"

## Objection Handling

### "We already have a POS."

Response:

"Good. I am not replacing it. I am using your menu and sales data to answer a different question: what should you change next to improve margin."

### "We already use spreadsheets."

Response:

"Then you already know the pain. The issue is not whether a spreadsheet can do the math. The issue is whether anyone updates it consistently and gets an action list from it."

### "We do not have time."

Response:

"That is exactly why this matters. The faster you see which items deserve action, the less time you waste reviewing the whole menu."

### "We do not want another software subscription."

Response:

"Then start with the audit. If the audit does not find real profit improvement, do not buy anything."

## Closing Paths

### Path A: Audit First

- free mini audit
- paid full menu audit
- convert to monthly subscription if value is clear

### Path B: Pilot First

- setup fee
- one-location pilot
- month-to-month subscription

## Early Commercial Strategy

Recommended first motion:

- consulting-led SaaS
- manual onboarding
- founder-run audit
- limited automation

Reason:

- faster learning
- lower build risk
- stronger customer insight
- better proof of willingness to pay

## What To Track In Sales

- number of owner conversations
- number of qualified restaurants
- audit acceptance rate
- demo-to-paid conversion rate
- objections by category
- time from first contact to yes or no
