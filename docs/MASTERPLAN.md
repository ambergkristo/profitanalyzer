# Menu Profit Optimizer Masterplan

## Purpose

Menu Profit Optimizer is a restaurant profit decision tool. Its job is not to run the restaurant. Its job is to tell the owner or manager which dishes are making money, which dishes are leaking margin, and what action should be taken next.

Core promise: increase restaurant profit by 10-20% without increasing sales volume.

## Customer

- Primary segment: independent restaurants and small groups with roughly 10-100 seats
- Primary buyer: owner or manager
- Primary user traits:
  - non-technical
  - time-poor
  - suspicious of complex software
  - willing to pay if the product produces obvious margin improvement fast

## Core Problem

Restaurants already have fragments of the truth:

- POS sales data
- inventory data
- recipes
- static reports

What they usually do not have is a decision layer that answers:

- Which dishes actually make money?
- Which dishes sell well but destroy margin?
- What changed when ingredient prices moved?
- What menu action should happen this week?

The real problem is not missing data storage. The real problem is missing decision clarity.

## Product Positioning

This product is:

- a profit optimization layer
- a menu economics lens
- a rules-based decision assistant for owners and managers

This product is not:

- a POS
- an inventory suite
- accounting software
- a general BI tool

Positioning line: We do not just show numbers. We tell you what to do.

## Product Thesis

If a restaurant can see dish-level profitability clearly and simulate the impact of pricing or recipe changes in minutes, the owner can make higher-quality menu decisions without Excel work, analyst help, or deep financial skill.

That thesis only holds if:

- input data is good enough
- onboarding is fast enough
- recommendations are specific enough to change behavior
- the output feels more like an action console than a spreadsheet

## Monetization

Initial pricing hypothesis:

- Starter: 29 EUR/month
- Pro: 59 EUR/month
- Multi-location: 99 EUR/month
- Setup fee: 100-300 EUR

Likely revenue model in practice:

- paid audit or setup-driven first sale
- recurring SaaS after first proof of value
- onboarding, data cleanup, and menu audit as upsells

## Commercial Reality

The product will not win on raw feature novelty alone. Current tools already cover recipe costing, menu engineering, price tracking, and broader back-office analytics. The wedge must be:

- simpler for small operators
- more action-oriented
- faster to first insight
- easier to buy than a full operations suite

## Major Risks

- restaurants do not maintain recipe and ingredient data consistently
- buyer pain is real but not urgent enough to trigger fast purchase
- existing spreadsheet workflows are ugly but acceptable to many operators
- the product drifts into a generic dashboard with weak recommendations
- customer acquisition becomes service-heavy and unscalable
- margin recommendations can be correct mathematically but ignored operationally

## Execution Principles

- Validation before build
- Action before analytics depth
- One obvious recommendation beats ten charts
- Manual-first onboarding before automation
- Narrow ICP before multi-location expansion
- Root-cause clarity over vanity dashboards
- Build the commercial demo before the full product

## Near-Term Goal

The first objective is not product breadth. The first objective is five paying restaurants that believe the tool helps them make better menu decisions than their current spreadsheet or POS report workflow.
