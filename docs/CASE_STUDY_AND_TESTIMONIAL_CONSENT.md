# Case Study And Testimonial Consent

Founding partner advocacy must be permission-based and value-based. This program must not create fake endorsements or pressure restaurants to post positive content.

## Principles

- No fake endorsements.
- No required positive posts.
- No public claim unless the partner confirms the product delivered real value.
- No use of restaurant name, logo, screenshots, quotes, or metrics without explicit permission.
- Lifetime access is a commercial relationship, not permission to publish private data.

## What Can Be Quoted

- Direct partner quotes approved by the partner.
- High-level workflow feedback approved by the partner.
- Case-study metrics only if the partner approves the exact numbers and context.

## What Must Be Anonymized

- Supplier names unless approved.
- Invoice images and raw invoice data unless explicitly approved.
- Internal recipes, exact ingredient costs, margin details, and revenue/sales data unless explicitly approved.
- Staff names and personal data unless explicitly approved.

## Screenshots

- Screenshots must be reviewed before publication.
- Blur or remove supplier, invoice, cost, staff, and private business details by default.
- Use synthetic/demo screenshots unless the partner explicitly approves real screenshots.

## Restaurant Name And Logo

- Use only with written permission.
- Define where the name/logo may appear: landing page, case study, social post, pitch deck, or founder outreach.

## Approval Workflow

1. Draft quote/case study/post.
2. Share exact text, screenshots, metrics, and placement with partner.
3. Get written approval.
4. Publish only approved material.
5. Keep approval record.

## Revocation And Correction

- Partners may request correction for inaccurate content.
- Removal requests should be handled promptly where commercially and legally feasible.
- Future use should stop if consent is revoked.
