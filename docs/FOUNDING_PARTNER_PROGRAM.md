# Founding Partner Program

## Purpose

The founding partner program remains a GTM layer, not the final product standard.

Profit Analyzer is now targeting production SaaS readiness. Founding partners are still useful, but they should be invited into a stronger product standard rather than visibly unfinished software.

This is not a paid endorsement program and not a shortcut to fake credibility.

## Target Partner Profile

Target restaurants should be:

- respected by peers
- run by owners or operators with visible credibility
- willing to give practical operational feedback
- open to testing menu and invoice workflows seriously
- credible enough that later public advocacy would matter

## Why 3-5 Only

- enough variety to surface real workflow issues
- small enough for founder-led onboarding and support
- small enough to keep product quality high
- large enough to see recurring patterns across restaurants

## What Partners Get

- lifetime access
- founder-led onboarding
- priority support
- early influence on workflow decisions
- a product shaped around real operator feedback

## What Partners Give

- real menu and invoice usage
- real feedback on setup, review, dashboard, and simulator
- honest reports on whether the product created value
- optional public advocacy or case-study permission only if value is proven

## Lifetime Access Terms

The product can offer lifetime access to founding partners as a relationship term.

That offer should be tied to:

- real participation
- good-faith usage
- feedback during the founding-partner phase

It should not be positioned as payment for praise.

The lifetime access promise should ultimately be represented inside the license or subscription model, not left as an undocumented handshake.

Phase 17 adds that representation:

- founding partners use an explicit `founding_partner_lifetime` entitlement
- the workspace subscription can resolve to `lifetime`
- notes can record why access was granted
- this still does not mean payment processing or public checkout is live

## Public Post And Case-Study Rules

Public references must follow these rules:

- no one is asked to post before they believe the product helped
- no fabricated outcomes
- no public use of a restaurant name without explicit permission
- case studies must be based on real product use
- any testimonial must be voluntarily approved by the partner

No fake endorsements, no purchased praise, and no performance claims without real usage evidence.

## Data Privacy And Trust Rules

- restaurant data stays confidential by default
- invoice and menu data must not be used publicly without permission
- screenshots or case-study numbers require explicit approval
- no blind OCR import is allowed
- review-confirm remains mandatory before cost mutation

## Mobile Relevance

Partners are likely to test invoice intake from a phone.

That means the product must be strong enough that:

- invoice upload works on mobile
- review-confirm works on mobile
- unresolved lines remain obvious on mobile
- dashboard actions remain readable on mobile

## Onboarding Process

1. founder screens restaurant fit
2. founder explains scope and limitations honestly
3. restaurant provides starter menu and cost data
4. workspace is set up
5. invoice intake flow is tested
6. dashboard and dish detail are reviewed together
7. follow-up feedback is collected

## Success Criteria

- partner completes setup
- partner completes at least one invoice review-confirm cycle
- partner understands top dashboard actions
- partner can identify at least one useful pricing or cost action
- partner is willing to continue using the product

## If The Product Does Not Deliver Value

If the product does not deliver value:

- the partner should not be pressured into a public post
- the founder should treat the feedback as product evidence
- the relationship should stay respectful and low-friction
- product learning matters more than forcing a testimonial
