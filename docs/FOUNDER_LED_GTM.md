# Founder-Led GTM

## Why The Previous Pilot Strategy Changed

The previous controlled pilot strategy produced a product that worked, but still felt too incomplete when shown to restaurant owners.

That created a problem:

- owners could not fully judge whether they needed it
- product incompleteness distorted feedback
- the product risked being rejected for polish and setup gaps rather than value

## New Launch Sequence

1. raise product quality toward production SaaS readiness
2. secure 3-5 credible restaurant partners
3. onboard them deeply
4. prove value through real usage
5. gain optional public advocacy only when earned
6. convert credible partner proof into wider demand capture

Public push should happen only after production SaaS readiness gates are met or consciously waived.

## Founding Partner Acquisition Plan

Target:

- 3-5 restaurant owners or operators
- each with credibility or influence in the restaurant scene

Selection criteria:

- respected operator reputation
- real menu and cost decision responsibility
- willingness to test invoice and setup flows
- ability to articulate product value publicly if earned

## Outreach Message

Core message:

- we are building a profit decision product for restaurants
- we want a small number of serious founding partner restaurants
- partners get lifetime access
- in return we want real usage and real feedback
- any public advocacy happens only if the product creates real value

## Onboarding Flow

1. intro call
2. confirm fit and expectations
3. collect starter menu and invoice data
4. set up workspace
5. run first invoice intake
6. review dashboard actions
7. gather friction and value feedback

## Public Post Strategy

Public content should only happen after:

- real product use
- real feedback
- clear partner permission

Public content can include:

- founder post summarizing what the partner achieved
- partner-written post
- short case study
- approved screenshot or quote

## Demand Capture

After real partner proof exists:

- update landing page
- show screenshots and action outcomes
- add case-study slots
- capture waitlist or demo requests

## Risk Controls

- do not ask for praise before value is proven
- do not push publicly with visibly unfinished software
- do not claim production SaaS readiness before the gates are actually met
- do not claim real OCR accuracy before benchmark evidence
- do not claim that influencer attention equals product-market fit

## What Not To Claim

- production SaaS readiness
- full OCR accuracy
- accounting or inventory completeness
- automated invoice trustworthiness without review
- broad market demand from a handful of partner relationships

Influencer-owner posts can create attention only if the product experience is genuinely strong.
## Phase 18 Launch Gate Rule

Founder-led GTM can continue only within the launch gate:

- controlled demo: allowed
- founding partner controlled launch: conditional
- public paid SaaS launch: no-go

Public advocacy must be based on real product value and explicit permission. Influential restaurant owners must not be pressured to post positive content, and case-study material must follow `CASE_STUDY_AND_TESTIMONIAL_CONSENT.md`.

No GTM material should claim production SaaS readiness until `PRODUCTION_LAUNCH_GATE.md` changes to go.
